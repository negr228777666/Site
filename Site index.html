Discover 320 years of history from swampy delta to cultural capital

Foundation (1703-1725)

Peter the Great's Window to Europe

  • Founded May 27, 1703 [1][2]
  • First structure: Peter and Paul Fortress [2]
  • Capital moved from Moscow in 1712 [1][2]
  • Architect Domenico Trezzini's European designs [1]

Imperial Capital (1725-1917)

Architectural Marvels Emerge

  • Winter Palace completed (1762)
  • Hermitage Museum founded (1764)
  • Alexander Column erected (1834) [1]
  • Bloody Sunday (1905)

Soviet Era (1917-1991)

Revolution and Resilience

  • 1917: October Revolution begins here [1][2][7]
  • 1924: Renamed Leningrad [1]
  • 1941-44: 872-day Siege of Leningrad [1]
  • 1955: Metro system opens

Modern City (1991-Present)

21st Century Renaissance

  • 1991: Original name restored [1]
  • 2003: 300th anniversary celebrations
  • 2017: Lakhta Center completed [1]
  • UNESCO World Heritage Site status [1]
